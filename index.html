围绕电商平台的产业链：线上加强线下集聚的证据

合作者：张莉，陈强远

《管理世界》已录用

摘要：链主型电商平台如何重塑传统产业链？本文以全球领先的跨境电商平台希音（SHEIN）为例，通过深入的实地调研，并结合中国工商企业注册、广东省纺织服装行业汇算清缴以及企业业务工序文本等多维微观大数据，考察了链主型平台入驻对周边产业链集聚与韧性的影响。研究发现：SHEIN的入驻显著加强了纺织服装企业在地理空间上的集聚，并呈现出影响随距离远近衰减，证明了数字时代地理邻近性在支撑"小单快反"模式中的重要性。在外部贸易冲击下，链主型平台通过强化产业链垂直分工、促进知识溢出及增强市场波动抵御能力，显著提升了周边企业的经营绩效与产业链韧性。

关键词：独角兽企业；SHEIN；集聚效应；跨境电商平台；链主企业

税制改革与以地谋发展模式转型：供需分析和政策优化

合作者：张莉，杨海生

《统计研究》第三轮R&R 修回

内容提要：当前，新一轮财税体制改革意义重大，重塑地方政府的以地谋发展行为和激发市场主体的高质量发展迫在眉睫。"营改增"是我国财税体制现代化的重大改革，本文以"营改增"政策为准自然实验，基于土地市场网2010年至2016年的微观土地出让信息和匹配了地块的税收调查数据，实证检验了此改革如何影响产业用地市场的供需双方，以及基于机器学习方法提出政策优化具体方向。结果表明，相比其他城市，试点城市在政策推行后工业用地的出让面积显著减少了10%，交易规模下降7%，实际单价上升5%，且效应在建设用地指标约束较强的城市中更为突出，在与服务业关联更紧密的行业以及质量较好的高等级地块更为明显。进一步地，采用因果路径分析考察了"财政激励效应"和"需求冲击效应"及二者的相对重要性，发现"营改增"使得制造业实际增值税率下降，这对政府而言是负向的财政激励，进而减少了出让工业用地培育增值税税源的动机；而在需求侧，这一税制改革使得制造业企业资源配置更合理，放缓了粗放式购置土地扩张厂房的动机，因此最终工业用地交易规模减少。最后，基于市场均衡处理效应框架的分析表明，工业用地的市场化交易与集约节约利用仍是未来改革的重点。本文的研究结论能为后续深化财税体制现代化改革以及城市土地配置发展目标提供优化方向。

关键词：税制改革；营改增；工业用地；因果路径分析；市场均衡下的处理效应

财政压力与地方政府住宅用地规模选择：开源和节流的双重视角

合作者：张莉，黎婉玉

内容提要：本文分析了财政压力对中国住宅用地"大地块"供给格局的影响，并揭示财政体制变革下地方政府土地治理的行为逻辑。以"营改增"为背景，研究发现在财政压力显著加剧时，地方政府往往通过扩大住宅地块单宗出让面积和强化地块溢价实现"开源"，同时有动机采用土地合同配建等方式将部分公共服务责任转嫁给市场主体，实现"节流"。2016年全面"营改增"后，出让大面积地块的数量和比例显著上升，住宅地价整体走高，特别是在竞争性拍卖模式下尤为明显。与此同时，上述模式强化了公共设施用地在"大地块"周围的集聚，削弱了城市公共资源的均衡分配。研究建议健全财税改革后的动态监测和绩效反馈机制，完善转移支付制度，统筹推进高质量城市空间治理与住房品质提升，实现财税稳定、土地有序供应与公共服务均等化。

关键词：住宅用地；大地块；财税改革；营改增

合作者：张莉，杨海生

进行中

Keeping Up with the Peers: The Race to the Bottom in Local Land Subsidies

Coauthored with: ZHANG Li, YANG Haisheng

Abstract: While there is a consensus that widespread competition exists among local governments within multi-layered large economies, the specific mechanisms of how this competition unfolds remain debated. Existing literature has predominantly focused on the instruments of competition rather than the process and its market implications. This paper investigates the behavioral logic and dynamic processes of local governments from a social interaction perspective, focusing on low-price subsidies for industrial land. Using tax survey data matched with micro-level industrial land transaction records, we explore the origins of the "race to the bottom" in land prices. We find that local government decisions regarding industrial land supply are not based solely on local factor endowments; rather, governments exhibit strong peer effects, mimicking and following the decisions of "peer" cities, often converging toward those offering the highest subsidies. Further analysis reveals that this behavioral response is more sensitive in key industries and high-tech sectors identified in planning documents, indicating significant industrial heterogeneity. Sub-sample analyses based on economic development levels and official turnover show that homogenous competition among local governments is complex, involving both imitative behavior and strategic adjustments driven by economic status and political cycles. Finally, we examine the economic consequences and find that this mimetic behavior distorts land supply without effectively enhancing firm productivity or promoting innovation.

Pains or Gains: The Spillover Effects of Superland in China

Coauthored with: ZHANG Li, LV Jinyi

Abstract: In the strategic context of mitigating external shocks and comprehensively expanding domestic demand, innovating and broadening physical consumption spaces has emerged as a crucial lever for facilitating the national economic cycle. However, the widespread development model of "mega-parcels" and gated communities—historically driven by rapid urbanization—has fundamentally constrained the effective release of consumption potential at the micro-level. This paper constructs a spatial general equilibrium model incorporating parcel characteristics. Exploiting a quasi-natural experiment generated by the 2009 residential land-use regulations and combining multi-source geospatial with micro-level POI data, we systematically identify the causal effects of super-large residential parcels on the local commercial ecosystem. The findings reveal that mega-parcels significantly suppress both the quantity and diversity of surrounding consumption amenities. The "barrier effect" of severing micro-level street networks heavily outweighs the "agglomeration effect" of population inflow. Our conclusions suggest that effectively boosting domestic demand urgently requires a shift away from the extensive land supply logic. As cities transition into an era of urban renewal and quality enhancement, policymakers must remain highly vigilant regarding spatial mismatch risks in lower-tier cities. Breaking down physical "islands" through refined governance—featuring smaller block sizes and denser street networks—will provide robust institutional and physical support for stimulating micro-commercial vitality and expanding high-quality consumption spaces.

Key Words: Land Development; Parcel Size; Spatial Equilibrium; Consumption Amenities
